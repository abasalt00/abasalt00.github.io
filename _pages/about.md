I'm a physics-trained engineer building hardware at the intersection of neuroscience, quantum sensing, and biomedical technology. I design experiments, build the instruments to run them, and turn hard science into deployable tools.

Currently at **UCLA Health**, I develop miniaturized, high-density electrophysiology systems for in vivo neural recordings in freely moving animals — combining custom PCB design, signal processing, and multimodal sensor integration.

I co-founded **[QuSpectra](https://github.com/quspectra)**, a deep tech startup awarded NASA SBIR funding to build a miniaturized quantum sensor for label-free molecular detection — a 50g device capable of detecting signals from samples too small for most instruments today.

---

## What I Build

- **Miniaturized neural recording systems** — 32-channel ephys + IMU + eye tracking, 1.07g total, for freely behaving mice
- **Quantum sensors** — NV-center magnetometry, miniaturized to fit in your hand, sensitive enough to detect disease before symptoms
- **Custom electronics end-to-end** — 6-layer PCBs, signal integrity, impedance matching, embedded firmware, mechanical enclosures
- **Full-stack software** — signal processing pipelines, data platforms, web applications
